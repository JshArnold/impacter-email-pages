MTSS Integration

Screening that maps to your existing support structure.

The Behavioral Health Screener doesn't create a new system — it makes your MTSS tiers more precise, more equitable, and faster to activate.

T1
Tier 1 — Universal Screening
All students, all grade bands, 3–5 prompts each
Every student completes voice-based prompts at the start of each semester. AI scoring identifies baseline patterns across all six behavioral health domains at the population level.
T2
Tier 2 — Targeted Support
Students flagged by signal patterns, counselor review
Students with elevated concern scores are surfaced automatically to counseling teams with domain-level evidence — not just a number, but the language that triggered the flag.
T3
Tier 3 — Intensive Intervention
High-need students, case coordination, documentation
For students with persistent or acute signals, screener data feeds directly into case documentation and tracks growth across re-screening intervals.

CYBHI Reimbursement

Districts can run this program at no net cost.

Through California's Children and Youth Behavioral Health Initiative (CYBHI), schools can submit for reimbursement on each behavioral health screening administered — effectively offsetting the cost of the program entirely.

$4.81
Reimbursement per administration under CPT 96127
$0
Net cost after CYBHI reimbursement for participating districts
Z13.89
Diagnosis code paired with CPT 96127 for behavioral health encounter billing

Our team handles documentation support to help districts configure their billing workflow. For most districts we work with, the screener pays for itself within the first screening window.

State Validation · BHSSA-002

Selected for California's statewide behavioral health evaluation.

In partnership with the Santa Clara County Office of Education, IMPACTER Pathway was selected by the California Behavioral Health Services Oversight and Accountability Commission (BHSOAC) for the statewide behavioral health performance management evaluation.

BHSSA-002 — Statewide evaluation of student behavioral health screening and outcomes. IMPACTER is one of a small number of district-facing tools selected for this validation study, which will inform California's behavioral health infrastructure for the next decade.
