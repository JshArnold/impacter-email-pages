One scoring frame. Four levels.
  • 01 Minimal

    Surface acknowledgment only. The student uses general or borrowed language — no personal connection, no evidence of internalized understanding. Screener flags for follow-up and tier consideration.

  • 02 Surface

    Descriptive but not reflective. The student names experiences or feelings but doesn't explain their significance. Language is transactional. Model notes emotional vocabulary present but unanchored.

  • 03 Engaged

    Personal narrative with self-awareness. The student connects experience to identity or growth. Specific language. The model finds patterns — metaphor, cause-and-effect framing, self-regulation signals.

  • 04 Reflective

    Integrated insight and agency. The student demonstrates nuanced understanding of self — including contradiction, uncertainty, or aspiration. Model flags resilience markers, help-seeking language, values clarity.

What Authentic Language Reveals That Numbers Can't

Student Response — NC Portrait · Adaptability

"When I think about a time I failed at something and how I grew from it... I think about my first year of JV soccer. I was cut because I wasn't good enough, and instead of giving up, I went to practice every single day on my own."

  • Resilience Marker

    Failure is recast as an unmet aspiration. The student doesn't catastrophize — they pivot to agency. High signal for Adaptability Level 3–4.

  • Help-Seeking Signal

    "On my own" indicates autonomous growth orientation — student did not disengage, but also did not seek support. Flag for educator awareness.

  • Language Pattern

    Specific temporal anchoring ("first year of JV") and behavioral detail ("every single day") correlate with Reflective Level scoring. Distinct from vague generalization.
