The Five Domains

What we measure.
What it means.

Each domain is a validated construct, scored against human expert benchmarks. Together they form a complete behavioral health profile for every student.

Domain 01 Self-Insight

A student's ability to recognize their own emotional states, identify patterns in their thinking, and articulate what they observe about themselves.

What it reveals
"I think I guard myself from failure — part of me understands it's the only way to truly succeed."
Domain 02 Emotional Resilience

How students navigate adversity, recover from setbacks, and sustain forward movement in the face of stress, pressure, or disappointment.

What it reveals
"I don't have as many failures as I expected — which made me realize I haven't taken enough risks."
Domain 03 Relational Awareness

The capacity to perceive and respond to the emotional states of others — the foundation for empathy, collaboration, and healthy peer relationships.

What it reveals
"When my friend was going through it, I noticed before they said anything — I just stayed close."
Domain 04 Conflict Resolution

Whether and how students approach interpersonal tension constructively — de-escalating, seeking understanding, and working toward repair.

What it reveals
"I usually just walk away. I don't know if that's handling it or avoiding it."
Domain 05 Help-Seeking

A student's willingness to recognize when they need support and their capacity to reach out to trusted adults or peers — the most predictive indicator of intervention success.

What it reveals
"I've never really told anyone when things are hard. I don't want to be a burden."
