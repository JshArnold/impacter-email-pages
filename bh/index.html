How it works

From prompt to support plan — in one session.

1
Student intake

Authentic voice, not forced choice

Students engage with a brief series of open-ended, spoken language prompts — no Likert scales, no checkboxes. Developed in partnership with the South County SELPA through SDCOE. Each prompt targets one of six behavioral health domains, delivered one at a time via an intuitive carousel interface.

Self-Insight
Emotional Resilience
Relational Awareness
Conflict Resolution
Effective Help-Seeking
Reflective Growth
2
NLP scoring

Real-time scoring against a validated rubric

Student audio or video responses are automatically transcribed and scored by IMPACTER's proprietary ML pipeline. Responses are evaluated against a research-validated four-point rubric grounded in engagement science (Amy Berry). Total score out of 24. Immediate MTSS tier assignment — no lag, no manual review required.

3
MTSS assignment

Evidence-based tier mapping at the moment of screening

Each score maps automatically to an MTSS support tier, with specific recommended next steps generated in real time. Tier 3 flags go directly to counselors. No bottleneck between screening and support connection.

4
Administrator dashboard

Individual review and schoolwide needs assessment

Guidance counselors and site admins access a dashboard surfacing all screener results, individual and aggregate trends, flagged responses for urgent review, and MTSS tier recommendations with suggested actions.

Emotional Resilience
Failure reframing

"My biggest failure would be not taking enough risk to experience true failure." The model flags this as a resilience marker: failure is recast as an unmet aspiration — not a catastrophe. There is no self-blame. The causal arrow runs forward, not backward.

Self-Insight
Avoidance pattern named

"I tend to stay in my comfort zone and stand behind the yellow caution tape." A spontaneous, original metaphor for her own avoidance behavior. The encoder isn't looking for key words — it's looking for the naming precision that signals genuine self-awareness. This is it.

Reflective Growth
Present-tense growth commitment

"Every day I'm trying to expand the barriers of my comfort zone... I'd like to experience a lot more failures." Present-tense growth orientation fused with a desire to increase risk exposure. She isn't describing past growth — she's narrating an active, ongoing process.

Implementation playbook

Seven battle-tested deployment models.

Every recommendation comes from real implementation experience. These aren't theoretical frameworks — they're approaches that work in actual schools with real constraints and staffing realities.

Download playbook (PDF)
1

Class Period Block — Homeroom / Advisory / SEL

The Classroom Champion. Universal coverage in 20–30 min. Grades 6–12. Consistent 1:1 device access. Minimal schedule disruption.

2

Mobile Assessment Cart — Indoor / Outdoor Pop-Up

The Flexibility Champion. Turn limited devices into an asset — roll a cart to any space. Works K–12. Elementary schools love this model.

3

Small-Group Testing Center — Wellness / Library / Lab

The Structured Sanctuary. Quiet, controlled environment. Groups of 10–20. Ideal for students who need calmer settings for authentic sharing.

4

Push-In Classroom Mini-Stations — Support / IEP-Friendly

The Seamless Support. Support staff arrives with 4–6 devices. Students rotate in small groups. No instructional time lost.

5

Pull-Out Targeted Sessions — Case Carrier / Support Team

The Personalized Care. ~15–20 min per student. Ideal for IEP/504 accommodations, ELL support, and students who work better in very small groups.

6

Family-Assisted Remote — Home Connection

The Home Connection. For absent students, hospital/homebound learners, or families who want to support participation. Flexible submission window.

7

Student-Guided Window — Self-Paced Completion

The Empowerment Approach. Students choose when, where, and how within a defined multi-day window. Ideal for large secondary campuses.
