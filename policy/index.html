What this makes possible

A new category of student data — in the hands of people who can act on it.

01
Board governance with real accountability
Districts using student outcomes focused governance now have behavioral health metrics worth monitoring at the board level — drawn from student voice, scored against competency rubrics, structured for public reporting.
02
Behavioral health data that reaches teachers
A student struggling with reading may have a persistence gap — not a phonics gap. A student disengaged from math may score low on curiosity. This data now reaches teachers and principals, not just counselors. The answer to academic problems isn't always academic.
03
A foundation for mandated behavioral health screening
California mandated dyslexia screening. A math screener is coming. The same logic — early identification, tied to outcomes — applies to behavioral health. Screened at 6th and 9th grade. Data flowing to ed services, not just counselors. The infrastructure now exists.
04
A better tool than anonymous, aggregated surveys
California's leading districts are moving from instruments that tell you how a district feels toward instruments that tell you how a student feels — individual, actionable, gathered in the student's own words. Not on a five-point scale.
The tool behind what you just watched

IMPACTER Pathway

IMPACTER is a voice intelligence platform built for K–12. Students respond to prompts in their own words — spoken, written, or in any of several languages. Every response is scored against eight research-based behavioral health competencies grounded in Harvard research and aligned to the CASEL framework.

The resulting data lives in district and teacher dashboards — structured, searchable, designed to inform wellness goals and academic intervention. Currently deployed across California, including every district shown above.
