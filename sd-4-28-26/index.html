Dr. Cooke & Mr. Derige,

As Rodney sets-up IMPACTER Sandbox access for the district's MTSS team, we're excited to share a first look at IMPACTER's Restorative Reflection Module — a workflow designed to leverage authentic student voice as a restorative tool within the MTSS framework. This module enables schools to create Restorative Scholar Profiles for students returning to class following behavioral incidents such as detention, Saturday school, suspension, or expulsion.

Developed in collaboration with Health Sciences High & Middle College (HSHMC) here in San Diego, this model reframes disciplinary moments as opportunities for reflection and growth.

Through six guided prompts, students respond by audio or video, expressing what happened, exploring intent vs. impact, and identifying supports for change. Each response is analyzed using Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Machine Learning (ML) to surface growth patterns across non-cognitive competencies validated by Harvard's Making Caring Common Project — including Self-Control, Perspective-Taking, Compassion, and Purpose.

The result is a data-rich, restorative narrative that teachers and counselors can view before re-entry conversations — strengthening trust, empathy, and connection.

Restorative Reflection Survey

Students complete a six-question reflection series designed to mirror the restorative process. Each prompt captures a different aspect of restorative growth:

  • 1
    Restorative Reflection #1 — Ownership & Transformation
  • 2
    Restorative Reflection #2 — Intent vs. Impact
  • 3
    Restorative Reflection #3 — Impact On Relationships
  • 4
    Restorative Reflection #4 — Natural Consequences & Restoration
  • 5
    Restorative Reflection #5 — Barriers & Support
  • 6
    Restorative Reflection #6 — Growth & Feedback

These reflections generate student-specific Restorative Reflection Analyses that evolve as responses accumulate.

The Science Behind the Signal

Like all applications of IMPACTER, every student reflection is processed through IMPACTER's NLP/ML pipeline, trained on billions of parameters of student responses aligned to our eight Anchor Attributes.

Using a DistilBERT-based transformer, our system identifies linguistic markers of empathy, accountability, and perspective-taking, assigning calibrated scores validated through:

  • Inter-Rater Reliability (IRR): Weighted Kappa values exceeding 0.84 (benchmark: human-to-human IRR in psychological research typically ranges 0.60–0.76)
  • 97.2% Adjacent Agreement with expert human scorers (±1 point on 5-point rubric; 82.4% exact match accuracy)
  • Mean Absolute Error (MAE): 0.18 points on 5-point scale — outperforming traditional automated scoring systems (85–92% adjacent accuracy) on the harder task of measuring character competencies
  • Ensemble scoring architecture: 70% embedding similarity + 20% lexical/syntactic features + 10% response sophistication sigmoid — preventing gaming via length or keyword stuffing

To prevent model drift, we maintain a locked "canary" dataset of 100 expertly-scored responses representing the full spectrum of competency levels and demographic contexts. Every week, we re-score the canary set with the current model; if accuracy drops below 95% adjacent agreement, an alert triggers human review.

In short, the science here is strong.
